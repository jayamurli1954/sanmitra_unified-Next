# LegalMitra Blog Editorial Standard

## Current State

LegalMitra has a separate Insights page and article pages under the LegalMitra frontend. Articles are currently stored as structured frontend data and rendered with source credits, a professional review note, and article imagery.

## Target State

LegalMitra blog articles should read like professional legal-tech publication content, not quick AI summaries. Each article should be credible enough for advocates, chartered accountants, company secretaries, founders, compliance teams, and educated citizens to treat it as a serious orientation note before seeking professional advice.

## Minimum Article Standard

Every LegalMitra article should include:

- A precise title with statutory or workflow context where applicable.
- Article identity metadata: author, reviewer, practice area, last updated date, reading time, and statutory/workflow tags.
- A strong opening that identifies the legal or compliance risk.
- Statutory or procedural anchors, including section numbers where relevant.
- A practical scenario or realistic professional workflow problem.
- A litigation, compliance, or editorial insight block that adds non-obvious practitioner judgment.
- A structured analysis with clear headings.
- A table, checklist, tracker format, or practitioner-ready framework.
- Source credits from official or authoritative sources.
- A professional disclaimer stating that the article is informational and not legal, tax, secretarial, or accounting advice.

## Prohibited Article Style

Do not publish articles that read like:

- Generic motivational summaries.
- Thin AI summaries without statutory grounding.
- LinkedIn-style commentary without source credits.
- Placeholder text with no practical examples.
- Articles that mention law broadly but omit procedure, evidence, limitation, filing risk, or compliance consequences.
- Repetitive AI cadence, such as every paragraph having the same length or every list item starting with the same phrase.
- Overuse of authority-signalling words such as `professional`, `premium`, or `expert` when the writing itself should demonstrate authority.

## Preferred Structure

Recommended structure:

1. Hero with title, subtitle, author, reviewer, practice area, updated date, reading time, and tags.
2. Editorial summary.
3. Key takeaways.
4. Statutory and practice anchors.
5. Litigation, compliance, or editorial insight.
6. Practical scenario.
7. Legal or compliance analysis.
8. Evidence, documentation, or filing checklist.
9. Common mistakes or risk notes.
10. Workflow recommendation.
11. Source credits.
12. Disclaimer.

## Quality Bar

A LegalMitra article should answer:

- What law, section, form, or procedure is being discussed?
- Why does it matter in real practice?
- What goes wrong when users ignore it?
- What documents or evidence should be preserved?
- What should a professional do next?
- Which sources support the article?
- Does the article contain at least one insight that a practitioner would recognise as coming from real workflow experience?
- Is the prose varied enough to avoid a generic AI-summary feel?

## Implementation Gap

The current implementation is frontend-data based. The target editorial workflow should eventually support:

- Backend-managed article content.
- Review status and author metadata.
- Source review dates.
- Article versioning.
- Optional legal reviewer approval before publication.
- Topic-specific templates for tax, criminal law, company law, contracts, compliance, and litigation strategy.

## Deferred Scope

The following are not part of the current LegalMitra E2E landing-page pass:

- CMS integration.
- Rich text editor.
- Legal reviewer workflow.
- Automated citation verification.
- Article publishing dashboard.
